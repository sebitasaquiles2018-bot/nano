# Characters

## Sebastian
**MALE.** Roman officer, late 20s. Athletic, soldier's build — broad shoulders, lean waist, strong forearms (he is a captain, not a model). Short, dark brown hair, trimmed practical Roman cut. Beardless or close-shaven shadow. Olive complexion, strong jaw, calm steady eyes. Eye color: dark brown.

Costume: Praetorian Guard officer uniform — bronze muscle cuirass (lorica musculata) over a deep red tunic, dark crimson cloak (sagum) pinned at the right shoulder, military caligae (sandal-boots) cross-laced to mid-calf, gladius at the left hip on a leather baldric. Crested helmet held under the arm or set down beside him in indoor scenes.

Manner: quiet authority. Does not posture. Stands very still.

In the arrows scenes (pages 8–10) he is bound to a wooden post in only a loincloth — historical accuracy of the icon. Body shows lean muscle, not idealized nudity. The arrows are the image — never the body for its own sake.

## Diocletian
**MALE.** Roman emperor, mid-50s. Stocky, bull-necked, weathered soldier's face. Short iron-grey beard, close-cropped grey hair, deep frown lines. Skin sun-darkened. Sharp dark eyes that do not blink.

Costume: imperial toga in deep crimson-purple with heavy gold embroidery at the hem; jeweled diadem (a beaded fillet, not a laurel wreath — historically Diocletian introduced the eastern diadem); heavy gold rings; senatorial sandals.

Manner: a man used to being obeyed. Anger is quiet at first. The face that breaks at the staircase confrontation.

## Irene of Rome
**FEMALE.** Christian widow, mid-30s. Modest, capable. Mid-brown hair pulled back under a dark headcovering (palla draped over the head). Plain dark blue or charcoal stola, no jewelry. Practical hands — she is a widow, not a noblewoman. Calm warm face.

## Chromatius
**MALE.** Prefect of Rome, early 60s. Heavy-set, gout-stricken (page 4 shows him before healing — swollen foot, walking-stick, pained expression). Full grey beard, balding crown, expensive senatorial toga with broad purple stripe (latus clavus). After healing his face transforms — relief, then awe.

## Mauretanian archers (group, no ref sheet)
Roman auxiliaries from north Africa. Lighter armor than the Praetorians, scale or leather, conical helmets with cheek-guards, composite recurve bows. Weathered, professional, indifferent. Three of them on page 8.
