# Settings

## Rome, c.286 AD
The Eternal City near the end of the 3rd century, midway through Diocletian's reign. Population ~1 million, vast and grimy. Marble palaces above, brick tenements below. Ongoing persecution: Christian gatherings are illegal, denouncement is rewarded.

## Specific locations

**The Praetorian camp (Castra Praetoria) — page 2.**
Massive brick fortress at the eastern edge of Rome. High walls, training ground, barracks. Lamps in iron sconces, oil-smoke air.

**A holding cell beneath a Roman magistrate's house — page 3.**
Low stone vault, single barred window, straw on the floor. Two or three Christian prisoners chained at the ankle. Sebastian comes by lamp-light.

**Chromatius's villa, atrium — pages 4–5.**
Grand Roman house. Marble floor, frescoed walls (faded reds and ochres), small impluvium (rain-pool) in the center, household shrine in a niche (which by page 5 has been *replaced* — old idols gone, a simple wooden cross or chi-rho symbol in their place).

**Diocletian's audience hall — pages 6–7, 12–13.**
The imperial throne room. Coffered ceiling far above, polished porphyry columns flanking a raised dais. Diocletian on a curule throne. Lictors in the shadows. Cold morning light from high windows.

**The field outside the Praetorian camp — pages 8–9.**
Open sandy ground used for archery practice. A wooden post driven into the dirt. Late afternoon light, long shadows, distant pines.

**Night, the same field — page 10.**
Same post, now in moonlight. Sebastian slumped against it, pierced. Irene approaches by lamp.

**A poor house near the Esquiline — page 11.**
Where Irene tends Sebastian. One small room, oil lamp, pallet bed, a curtained doorway.

**The palace staircase — pages 13–14.**
Wide marble flight rising to the imperial wing. Diocletian descends; Sebastian waits below. The confrontation happens here, in cold morning light.
