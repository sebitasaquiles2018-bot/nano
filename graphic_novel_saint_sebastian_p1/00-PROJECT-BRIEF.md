# Saint Sebastian: The Officer Who Walked Back

**Series:** Bio (kid-driven, named-saint) — companion to Genghis historical bio.
**Subject:** Sebastian of Rome (c.255–288 AD), captain in Diocletian's Praetorian Guard, Christian martyr.
**Length:** ~14 inner pages + 1 cover = 15 pages.

## Arc
- **Before:** Trusted captain in Diocletian's Praetorian Guard, secretly Christian, protecting and converting fellow Romans.
- **Turn:** Denounced. Bound to a post, shot through with arrows by Mauretanian archers, left for dead. Found alive by Irene of Rome, who nurses him back.
- **After:** Healed, walks straight back into the palace, blocks Diocletian on the staircase, accuses him to his face. Clubbed to death.

## Documented vs. legend
- **Documented:** Existed, martyred at Rome under Diocletian's persecution. Listed in the Chronograph of 354 (feast Jan 20), cult attested by Ambrose c.386–390.
- **Legend (Passio Sancti Sebastiani, 5th c.):** Birthplace Narbonne, education Milan, joined army 283, captain of Praetorian Guard, conversions of Chromatius/Tiburtius/Nicostratus/Claudius, arrows by Mauretanian archers, healing by Irene of Rome, return to confront Diocletian, beating with cudgels, body thrown in the Cloaca Maxima.
- The book follows the Passio because that *is* the cultural memory of the saint. We don't pretend the arrows are documented — we tell the story everyone tells, with care.

## Tone
Serious but human. The icon shows a beautiful naked youth pierced by arrows; the man underneath was a Roman officer who used his rank to comfort prisoners and then walked into the palace knowing what would happen. Find the person inside the icon.
